Amazon Web Services (AWS) Identity and Access Management (IAM) account requests

How to avoid common delays

For the Cloud Vending Team to be able to complete an Amazon Web Services (AWS) Identity and Access Management (IAM) ticket, it needs to have the right information available within it. Once you are confident you understand what is required you can find guidance on how to create an IAM ticket.

You will never be able to provide too much information, but you can provide too little. By failing to give us enough information could result in a delay in the completion of your ticket. The Cloud Vending team will only be able to work from the information you provide, it is not our responsibility nor is it secure or appropriate for us to guess what is required.

To assist you we wanted to highlight some of the common issues we have experienced.

  1. Missing or inaccurate approval – the Cloud Vending Team works from an agreed and verified approvers list. See Account roles and approvers for more information.
  2. If the user you are requesting a new account for, or a password reset for, has an ‘@digital.homeoffice.gov.uk’ email address you must provide us with their GPG key. We need this to encrypt their credentials before sending them out.
  3. Change ‘Issue Type’ to ‘AWS Account Change Request’ - if this is not done your IAM ticket will appear in our other backlog and may not be picked up straight away.
