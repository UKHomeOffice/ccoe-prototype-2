Recharging in the Home Office

Networks and Infrastructure (N&I) pay the monthly bills for Amazon Web Services (AWS). We then recharge each business area and project for their usage of AWS services. We recharge for all elements of cloud spend in the Home Office. This includes AWS Usage on your own accounts, ACP platform usage & support and Azure Usage.

Why we recharge

There are two reasons why we recharge:

  • To maintain a single route for billing on AWS. This helps us make sure there is central visibility of spending across the Home Office. Which helps us stay within our contractual limits for spending on AWS. This also means that we only have one contractual relationship with AWS. So, we can leverage for joint discounts and coordination of added benefits.
  • Making each business area responsible for their costs allows you to view and understand their spending on AWS. We want to support you in taking ownership of your own costs and driving cost efficiencies.

AWS cost and recharge breakdown

Monthly AWS usage

We bill your business area for all usage in your AWS account. You are in control of your monthly bill. It will increase and decrease depending on the number of resources in your account and your cost optimisation activity. We bill your usage in pounds but, AWS costs are calculated using dollars. So, there may be fluctuations to the exchange rate throughout the year which effect final recharging.

Cost to your business area

Each month we take usage figures for each account you own from the AWS console. These figures are excluding VAT and take into account discounts that we pass onto business areas such as a central savings plan.

Monthly infrastructure charge

N&I are responsible for maintaining a minimum level of infrastructure required for the wider HO to use AWS services. This consists of direct connections with AWS, firewalls and VPN & VPC connections. This charge can vary as the central infrastructure costs are split amongst all tenants on AWS. If the pool of tenants changes then the central cost proportion will vary.

Cost to your business area

This is not recharged to your business area. The cost of this is now covered by Volume Commitment Programme discount. Compared to the recharging process in financial year 19/20, this represents a reduction of 0.2% of your overall monthly bill.

AWS enterprise/premium support

All AWS accounts should have premier support activated due to being an enterprise customer. This allows you to access the support function within your AWS account and receive prod critical responses within 15 minutes. This cost also provides the home Office access to an AWS Account Team. This includes a Technical Account Manager, a Solutions Architect & a Customer Success Manager.

Cost to your business area

This is not recharged to your business area. The cost of enterprise support (shown as Premier Support in the AWS console) is charged directly to the Home Office. We pay this bill on your behalf by using savings from the Volume Commitment Programme. Compared to the recharging process in financial year 19/20, this represents a reduction of 5.2% of your overall monthly bill.

Monthly management overhead

To support the stand up of a public cloud team we charge a 2% management overhead on top of your combined usage and infrastructure charge figure. This 2% covers a central team who can support you with Cost Optimisation, Security Alerting & Account Management.

Cost to your business area

This is not recharged to your business area. We cover this cost by using savings from the Volume Commitment Programme. Compared to the recharging process in financial year 19/20, this represents a reduction of 2% of your overall monthly bill.
