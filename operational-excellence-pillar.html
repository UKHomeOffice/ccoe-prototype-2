    Operational excellence

    Operational excellence pillar

    The operational excellence pillar focuses on how to support development and run workloads effectively. Also, understand organisations and their best practices. There is currently bespoke Home Office guidance on AWS organisations and Tagging standards. To give you an oversight of the pillar we have included the design principles below. For more information on the operational excellence pillar view the further resources:

    • AWS operational excellence pillar
    • Microsoft Azure operational excellence pillar

    Design principles

    Perform operations as code

    In the cloud, you can apply the same engineering discipline that you use for application code to your entire environment. You can define your entire workload (applications, infrastructure, etc.) as code and update it with code. By performing operations as code, you limit human error and enable consistent responses to events.

    Make frequent, small, reversible changes

    Design workloads to allow components to be updated regularly. Make changes in small increments that can be reversed.

    Refine operations procedures frequently

    As you use operations procedures, look for opportunities to improve them. As you evolve your workload, evolve your procedures appropriately. Set up regular game days to review and validate that all procedures are effective and that teams are familiar with them.

    Anticipate failure

    Perform “pre-mortem” exercises to identify potential sources of failure so that they can be removed or mitigated. Test your failure scenarios and validate your understanding of their effect. Test your response procedures to ensure they are effective and that teams are familiar with their execution. Set up regular game days to test workload and team responses to simulated events.

    Learn from all operational failures

    Make improvement through lessons learned from all operational events and failures. Share what is learned across teams and through the entire organisation.
